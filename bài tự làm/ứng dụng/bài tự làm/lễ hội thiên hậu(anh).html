Thien Hau Temple Festival opens every year on March 23 and September 9 (lunar calendar), to celebrate Lam Tuc Mac's birthday and transformation. The festival attracts a large number of Chinese families in Pho Hien and Vietnamese to sacrifice. Thien Hau Temple (Thien Hau Upper Street) located on Trung Trac Street - Quang Trung Ward - Hung Yen Town was built in 1640 by 40 Chinese families in Guangdong, Guangxi and Fujian with donations for construction. Candlestick. Thien Hau Temple worships Lam Tuc Mac, according to "Dai Nam Nhat Thong Chi", she is a sea god. Lam Tuc Mac was born on March 23 (lunar calendar) as the 6th daughter of Lam Nguyen, Bo Dien, Fujian province (China). Legend has it that when Lam Tuc Mo was born, he had a sweet fragrance and a brilliant aura. Lam Tuc Mo is very smart, when he was 8 years old, he went to study fairies, practiced singles to become true results, could call rain to call the wind, and used magic to ride a mat in the sea. When the people lost their crops and were hungry, she found seaweed to eat instead of rice, noodles, and barley, so the people were no longer hungry. She found a kind of magic oil, sprayed on the ground to grow seeds to eat instead of rice… On the 9th day of the lunar calendar, she did not get sick but turned. After transforming, she often wears a red shirt and flies over the sea to help ships pass by. The Fujian people respect her as the god of the Sea, wherever there are Fujian people, there is a temple to worship her. When migrating to Pho Hien, the Fujian people built a temple to worship her on Bac Hoa Street (now Trung Trac Street). Thien Hau Temple is a work with bold Chinese architectural colors on many items such as: Nghi Mon gate, incense burning house, roof, corner blade and truss structure. Legend has it that the temple was made in China and then brought to Pho Hien to build. Nghi Mon is built like a house, the roof is tiled with pipes, the architecture of the sets is because of the stacking style, the door system is engraved with the image of the mandarin, martial arts and the servants. The floor is paved with cobblestone slabs that have not worn down through the rain and wind for hundreds of years. In front of the gate, there are a couple of clams: the male holds the pearl, the female hugs the baby, the material is granite, the artifact is very vivid. The interior was built in the style of nail, including the incense burning house and the harem. The incense crematorium has a stack of matches on 2 floors and 8 roofs, the roof is covered with pipe tiles, the roof line is decorated with the Buddha sitting on a lotus, surrounded by passersby. The whole system is structured in a special shape with each Chinese theme and story such as: Three Kingdoms, Journey to the West... In addition, it is also decorated with string flowers, bowls of pants, scenes. four seasons: Spring, Summer, Autumn, Winter. The harem consists of 3 compartments, with a "winged stack" architecture, on the excess heads carved into the shape of a carp. The harem room, in addition to worshiping Lam Tuc Mac, also worships her parents, siblings and the Chinese clans who have built the temple. Every year, Thien Hau Temple opens on March 23 and September 9 (lunar calendar) to celebrate Lam Tuc Mac's birthday and transformation. A large number of Chinese families in Pho Hien and Vietnamese people come to worship. During the festival, a royal procession is held. The offerings include seaweed cake, turtle cake, To Chau cake, etc., which are traditional products of the Chinese people. Thien Hau Temple is one of the very few ancient Chinese architectural works in Pho Hien still preserved to this day. For the purpose of preserving and propagating to the masses, Hung Yen province restored Thien Hau Temple to be spacious and clean and in 1992 was recognized by the Ministry of Culture and Sports as an artistic architectural monument.
